About me

My name is Jaya, I use they/them pronouns, and I am in my final year at Mount Holyoke College studying astronomy and biological sciences.

My research covers both astronomy and ecology. I have spent two years studying low surface-brightness galaxies, their composition, and their structure, and I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in astronomy. In the summer of 2021, I participated in an NSF-REU at the University of Virginia's Blandy Experimental Farm, in Boyce, VA. There, I studied the foraging patterns of Bombus impatiens, the common eastern bumble bee.

I have been a coxswain since 2014 and sports have been one of the most central aspects of my life since then. At Mount Holyoke, I have overseen, assisted, and created myriad efforts within the athletics department focused on diversity and inclusion.
