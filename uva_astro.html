Galactic interactions and mergers

The driving question: what happens when you smash a couple galaxies into each other?

I work with Prof. Aaron Evans and the GOALS Collaboration studying galactic interactions across the universe. We observe Luminous and Ultra-Luminous Infrared Galaxies (LIRGs and ULIRGs, respectively) with instruments covering the entire electromagnetic spectrum.

IRAS F09111-1007

Hubble Space Telescope optical image of IRAS F09111-1007.

I am currently doing a multiwavelength deep-dive of the power sources driving the interaction seen above. This interaction is unusual for many reasons, one of which is that it's peculiar to have a ULIRG with such neat, un-disturbed spiral structure and no active merging partner.

So far, I have measured the stellar mass of each galaxy and confirmed that all four galaxies in this system are, in fact, interacting with each other! I have also determined the source of ionization at the nucleus of each galaxy. Now, I am studying the central stellar kinematics to place constraints on the AGN of the system, and eventually measure the mass of the black holes in the LIRG and ULIRG. These measurements will be compared with constraints on the AGN given X-ray data from the NuSTAR telescope.

This work will be the basis of my M.S. (expected 2024), and provide the backbone of my Ph.D. (expected ~2027).
