Mentorship in and outside of academic spaces

Fimbel Maker and Innovation Lab

The Fimbel Maker and Innovation Lab at Mount Holyoke College is an 8,000-square-foot Makerspace. It is completely student-run every evening, and I have been working there since 2019.

In Fimbel, we are required to have a baseline of technical knowledge deeper than what is necessary in any other space on campus. It is impossible to be intimidated by new equipment when you have to teach it to someone almost immediately after learning it. Every day, someone walks in with an idea that would have never occurred to me, and I must be resiliently creative enough to arm them with the skills to figure it out. In my first semester, I helped a student design and build a full Settlers of Catan game board. That spring, I assisted my first workshop (about 3D modeling a chocolate bar), and I cut a fleet of planks with a student who had designed a composter for her research. Within the year, I was preparing to lead a laser cutting workshop for a group of 15 people, none of whom had any experience. All of the people who I have worked with have slowly become more confident in their abilities, and it is gratifying to see that my efforts to make the space less intimidating for newcomers have been successful. In concert with my other mentorship positions, the Fimbel Lab has allowed me to teach people new skills and improve my capacity for growth and creative learning, which has been incredibly fun.

Some of the skills skills that I am trained on and qualified to teach:
  • The use of Adobe Illustrator for laser cutting.
  • Laser cutter use and maintenance.
  • The use of various 3D modeling and CAD softwares for 3D printing.
  • 3D printing with both FDM extrusion and stereolithography printers.
  • Sewing with generic, industrial, and vintage machines.
  • Vinyl cutting (sticker vinyl and heat vinyl).
  • Soldering for electronics and jewelry.
  • Various wood and metal-shop tools.

Peer-led undergraduate mentoring system (PLUMS)

PLUMS Mentors are responsible for creating weekly worksheets, running semiweekly discussion sections for students, and assisting weekly laboratories.

Courses I have been a PLUMS Mentor for:
  • Ecology, Fall 2021
  • Calculus-based Physics: Mechanics, Fall 2020
